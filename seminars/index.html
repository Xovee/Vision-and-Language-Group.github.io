Speaker: Zijie Huang [PPT (password: iccy)]
Topic:Adversarial Attack Based On Disrupting Image Feature
Date:April 24, 2020
Abstract: Research on adversarial images to date has focused mainly on disrupting classification, i.e., on algorithms that produce images classified with labels that are patently inconsistent with human perception. Given the large, potentially unbounded regions of feature space associated with a given class label, it may not be surprising that it is easy to disrupt classification. Today this report will introduce two papers at first, and further introduce my work.

Speaker: Ning Kang [PPT (password: b18v) ]
Topic:Pose-guided Occluded Person Re-ID
Date:April 24, 2020
Abstract: Person re-identification (Re-ID) aims to retrieve a probe pedestrian from non-overlapping camera views, which is widely used in smart video surveillance. However, persons can be easily occluded by some obstacles (e.g. baggage, cars, counters, trees). In order to match persons with occluded images, most existing methods focus on alignment between occluded image and non-occluded images based on part features. Besides, pose information is also efficient for occluded person Re-ID. This report will introduce two pose-guided alignment methods:
(1) Pose-guided Visible Part Matching for Occluded Person ReID
(2) High-Order Information Matters: Learning Relation and Topology for Occluded Person Re-Identification
