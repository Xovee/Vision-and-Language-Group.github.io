Speaker: Ning Kang [PPT&Video(password: 1234) ]
Topic:Person Re-identification (Re-ID)
Date:July 17, 2020
Abstract: Person Re-identification (Re-ID) aims at identifying the same persons' images across different cameras and achieves high performance on large-scale datasets. However, when deploying such re-id models to new domains, their performances often degrade significantly, mainly due to the inevitable domain gaps between datasets collected from different surveillance camera networks. Existing domain adaptation methods for Re-ID can be divided into two main categories, including domain translation-based methods and pseudo label-based methods. This report will introduce two state-of-the-arts works.

Speaker: Zhilong Zhou [PPT&Video(password: 1234) ]
Topic:PointRend: Image Segmentation as Rendering
Date:July 17, 2020
Abstract: By analogizing classical computer graphics methods for efﬁcient rendering with over- and undersampling challenges faced in pixel labeling tasks, the author develop a unique perspective of image segmentation as a rendering problem. From this vantage, the paper presents the PointRend (Point-based Rendering) neural network module: a module that performs point-based segmentation predictions at adaptively selected locations based on an iterative subdivision algorithm. PointRend can be ﬂexibly applied to both instance and semantic segmentation tasks by building on top of existing state-of-the-art models.
