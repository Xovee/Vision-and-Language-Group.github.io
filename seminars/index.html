Speaker:Tangming Chen [PPT&[Video(password: 1234)]
Topic:Graph for Visual Dialog
Date:July 31, 2020
Abstract: Visual dialog is a challenging task that requires the comprehension of the semantic dependencies among implicit visual and textual contexts. With the development of graph networks, more and more papers use graph to solve the problem. In this presentation, I will introduce two papers which use dense graph and sparse graph respectively in visual dialog.

Speaker:Qilong Zhang [PPT&Video(password: 1234) ]
Topic:Generator-based attacks
Date:July 31, 2020
Abstract: Deep neural networks (DNNs) have achieved great successes in various vision applications due to their strong expressive power. However, recent studies have shown that DNNs are vulnerable to adversarial examples which are manipulated instances targeting to mislead DNNs to make incorrect predictions. In this presentation, I will give an introduction about a series of attacks which are based on generator networks. Besides, we will also introduce the reciprocal relationship between GAN and attacks.
