Speaker:Cheng Chen [PPT&Video(password: 1234)]
Topic:Referring Expression Comprehension
Date:Dec 18, 2020
Abstract: Referring expression comprehension is a ubiquitous building block in many vision-language tasks and yet remains challenging due to large variations in visual and linguistic features of grounding entities, strong context effect and the resulting semantic ambiguities. Prior works typically focus on learning representations of individual phrases with limited context information. To address their limitations, this paper proposes a language-guided graph representation to capture the global context of grounding entities and their relations, and develop a cross-modal graph matching strategy for the multiple-phrase visual grounding task.
