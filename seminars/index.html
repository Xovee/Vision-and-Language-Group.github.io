Speaker: Haoyu Wang [PPT (password: a069)]
Topic:Video Captioning
Date:May 22, 2020
Abstract: Video captioning task usually uses scene-level and object-level informations. Existing captioning models always generate sentences like “a man …”. These model pay few attention on object interactions. Recently, Two papers from CVPR2020 use graph to exploit relationships between object and object. Two parts are included in my report:
（1）Object-Oriented Captioning
（2）Use Spatio-Temporal Graph for video captioning

Speaker: Yaya Chen [PPT (password: e20m) ]
Topic:adversarial attack and defense against Semantic segmentation
Date:May 22, 2020
Abstract: Deep neural networks can be easily fooled by ‘adversarial examples’. Current attack algorithms that seek to enhance adversarial transferability work on the decision level.This leads to two key limitations: (a) An attack is dependent on the task-specific loss function, (b) The adversarial examples are specific to the network architecture and demonstrate poor transferability to other network architectures. two parts are included in my report:
(1) a novel approach to create adversarial examples that can broadly fool different networks on multiple tasks.
(2) the initial attempt to explore the defense strategy on semantic segmentation by formulating a general adversarial training procedure that can perform decently on both adversarial and clean samples.
